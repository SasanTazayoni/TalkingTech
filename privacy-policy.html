Lawful bases and data protection rights

Under UK data protection law, we must have a "lawful basis" for collecting and using your personal information. You can find out more about lawful bases on the ICO's website.

Your data protection rights include:

  • Right of access — you can ask for copies of your personal information.
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to erasure — you can ask us to delete your personal information.
  • Right to restriction of processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your information.
  • Right to object — you can object to the processing of your personal data.
  • Right to data portability — you can ask us to transfer your information to another organisation or to you.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To make a data protection rights request, please contact us using the details above. We must respond within one month.

How long we keep information

User account data is held for as long as the account is active, plus 2 years after the last interaction. Contact form enquiries are held for 1 year. After these periods, personal data is securely deleted.

Who we share information with

We use the following data processors who handle personal information on our behalf:

  • Calendly — processes appointment booking information on our behalf.
  • Formspree — processes contact form submissions on our behalf.
